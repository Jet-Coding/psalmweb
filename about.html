PHILIPPINE STUDENT ALLIANCE LAY MOVEMENT INC.

The Philippine Student Alliance Lay Movement, Inc., officially referred to as PSALM, is a specialized Christian ministry committed to discipling the students and the laity for the Lord Jesus Christ.

It started from a small and humble beginning. It evolved from a College Youth Center (CYC) which was established on August 8, 1969 in Zamboanga City, Philippines. Later on February 22, 1977, this ministry became a national movement and duly registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission.

It is an interdenominational Christian organization working among students and the lay. PSALM is now in almost all key cities, municipalities, and provinces throughout the Philippines, namely: Zamboanga City, Ipil, Buug, Pagadian City, Ozamiz City, Dipolog City, Davao City, Tagum City, Mati, Cagayan de Oro City, Bukidon (Malaybalay & Valencia), Butuan City, Iligan City, Cotabato City, Midsayap (North Cotabato), General Santos City, Sultan Kudarat, Koronadal, Kidapawan City, Arakan, Kabacan, Cebu, Dumaguete City, Iloilo City, Bacolod City, Sogod (Southern Leyte), Naval (Eastern Leyte), Tagbilaran City (Bohol), Metro Manila, Naga City, Romblon and Puerto Princesa (Palawan).

PSALM, as a movement, believes that when we reach a student, we reach a family; when we reach a family, we reach a community ; and when we reach a community, we transform the world. READ MORE

HISTORY OF PSALM

1969

Dr. Joseph Arthur established the College Youth Center (CYC) on August 8, 1969 in Zamboanga City, Philippines.

1974

Later on 1974, this ministry became a national movement and duly registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission as the Philippine Student Alliance Lay Movement, Inc. (PSALM).

1978

PSALM began to extend, expand, and enlarge by opening its first MISSION PROJECT in Metro Manila in 1974 and it second MISSION PROJECT in Dumaguete City. Though not a mission project, the work North Cotabato was also opened and pioneered by the Delegetes who attended the NLTI in 1978.
